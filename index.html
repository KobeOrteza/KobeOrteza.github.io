About Me

I’m Kobe Lean P. Orteza — a student of BS Artificial Intelligence Engineering with a passion for building practical robotics and ethical AI systems that help communities. I first discovered robotics in Grade 10 and since then I’ve been driven to apply AI in tangible ways, from hardware prototypes (Arduino) to mobile applications. I chose AI Engineering because it lets me combine software, hardware, and real-world problem solving.

What I believe

  • Good AI should be transparent, inclusive, and built for people — not just algorithms.
  • Robotics and low-cost automation can have a huge impact in agriculture and public health.
  • Learning by building is the fastest way to master engineering skills.

Learning Embedded ML — Oct 2025

Short reflection

This month I focused on running a small neural network on an edge MCU. The tricky parts were model quantization and optimizing sensor read loops for responsiveness. Key takeaway: measure power and latency early; optimizing code is as important as improving the model.

Why ethical AI matters in student projects — Sep 2025

Short reflection

Even in class projects, biased datasets can produce unfair outcomes. I started running simple bias checks (class balance, confusion matrices by subgroup) and it changed how I design data collection for the Ethical AI Framework project.
